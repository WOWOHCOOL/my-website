The Ultimate Factory Verification Checklist for Power Accessory Brands

A low price quote is meaningless if the factory lacks the infrastructure for quality. Whether you are sourcing high-power GaN Chargers or magnetic Wireless Pads, use this 2026 audit guide to separate professional manufacturers from small trading workshops.

Apr 21, 2026 12 min read

Sourcing from China can be incredibly profitable, but it comes with significant risks that can wipe out your investment overnight. From fake factories posing as legitimate manufacturers to counterfeit certifications and outright scams, the stakes are high for any importer entering the Chinese supply chain for the first time.

According to industry data, approximately 30% of first-time China importers experience supplier-related issues within their first year. Many of these problems could be prevented with proper due diligence using a systematic factory verification process. Whether you're sourcing wireless chargers, power chargers, or any other consumer electronics, the verification principles remain the same.

This comprehensive checklist covers everything you need to verify before partnering with any Chinese manufacturer—from basic documentation to factory audits, quality control systems, and social compliance standards. Think of this guide as your roadmap to building a reliable, long-term partnership with a verified factory that will protect your brand reputation and your bottom line.

We've organized this checklist into logical phases, so you can work through it systematically whether you're evaluating suppliers remotely or conducting an on-site factory audit. Each section includes practical actions you can take immediately, regardless of your experience level.

Core Production Line Audit: By Product Category

Different power accessory categories require specialized verification points. Use this category-specific checklist to audit factories for your specific product type:

Power Bank Verification Points

R&D & Tooling

  • • Private mold (private tooling) design capability
  • • Battery cell sourcing partnerships (Samsung, LG, ATL)
  • • PCB layout and protection circuit design

Production & QC

  • • Fire-proof Li-ion cell storage area
  • • UN38.3 testing capability
  • • OQC (Outgoing Quality Control) records

Wireless Charger Verification Points

R&D & Tooling

  • • Qi/WPC certification database listing
  • • FOD (Foreign Object Detection) testing equipment
  • • Magnetic alignment fixture calibration

Production & QC

  • • Coil winding machines (automated preferred)
  • • Qi test stations for certification
  • • Temperature cycling chambers

GaN Charger Verification Points

R&D & Tooling

  • • High-frequency transformer design capability
  • • Thermal management engineering
  • • PD protocol integration (Navitas, Pi, etc.)

Production & QC

  • • Chroma load testing machines
  • • Thermal cameras for heat dissipation
  • • 4-hour minimum aging test rooms

Car Charger Verification Points

R&D & Tooling

  • • Multi-port PD protocol support
  • • Vehicle integration testing capability
  • • Over-voltage/over-current protection design

Production & QC

  • • Salt spray testing equipment (corrosion)
  • • Load testing for all ports simultaneously
  • • 12V/24V vehicle compatibility testing

Document Verification Checklist

Before engaging deeply with any supplier, you must verify their legal existence and business credentials. This foundational step prevents most common scams and ensures you're dealing with a legitimate business entity that can be held accountable.

Certification Authenticity Check

Counterfeit certifications are rampant in Chinese manufacturing. A factory may display impressive certificates on their website or in communications, but many are forged, expired, or don't cover the products you're sourcing. Verifying certifications independently is non-negotiable for protecting your business.

  • • FCC SDoC Database - Verify US radio frequency compliance
  • • CNAS Database - Verify Chinese accreditation bodies

Production Facility Inspection Points

Understanding a factory's actual production capability is essential for forecasting, quality assurance, and avoiding delays. Use this checklist during a video tour or in-person audit to assess whether the factory can handle your volume requirements consistently.

Quality Control Verification

Quality control separates professional factories from commodity suppliers. A robust QC system isn't just about catching defective products—it's about preventing defects from occurring in the first place through systematic process control.

Financial Stability Assessment

A factory's financial health directly impacts their ability to fulfill long-term orders, invest in quality equipment, and maintain consistent production. Assessing financial stability helps you avoid partners who may go bankrupt mid-order or cut corners to stay afloat.

Social Compliance Audit

Social compliance is increasingly critical for Western markets. Brands face reputational risk and potential legal liability for supply chain labor practices. A comprehensive social compliance audit protects your brand and ensures ethical production standards.

Communication & Professional Assessment

Communication quality often predicts relationship success. Professional factories maintain clear, responsive communication as a standard practice. Use these checkpoints to evaluate their communication and professionalism.

Sample Evaluation Process

Samples are your best preview of production quality. Never skip sample evaluation, regardless of how impressive a factory's credentials appear. This phase reveals the truth about their actual manufacturing capability.

Red Flags: Warning Signs to Watch

Recognizing warning signs early saves you from costly mistakes. If you encounter multiple red flags, reconsider the supplier regardless of how attractive their pricing or credentials appear.

Refuses to provide any documentation

Legitimate factories are registered and can provide business licenses, certifications, and references. Refusal to share basic documentation is a major warning sign.

Prices too good to be true

If pricing is 30%+ below market rates, something is wrong—usually quality, authenticity, or there's no actual goods to deliver. Get multiple quotes and understand market pricing.

No sample policy or refuses samples

Professional manufacturers stand behind their products with samples. Refusal to provide samples indicates they either can't manufacture to your specs or have something to hide.

Poor communication or delayed responses

Communication problems during the sales phase multiply after payment. If they can't respond promptly now, expect worse after you've committed money.

Can't verify certifications independently

If a factory claims certifications but you can't verify them through official channels, assume they're fake. Always verify independently.

Only accepts Western Union or informal payments

Professional factories accept bank transfers, letter of credit, and payment terms through established financial channels. Western Union is for personal transactions, not B2B manufacturing.

Asks for full payment upfront with no protection

Reputable factories offer payment terms that protect both parties. 100% advance payment removes all your leverage and risk protection.

Pressure tactics and limited-time offers

Legitimate suppliers don't pressure you into quick decisions. "Order now or lose the price" tactics indicate desperation or fraud, not professional business relationships.
