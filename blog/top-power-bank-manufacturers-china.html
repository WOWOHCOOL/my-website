4. How to Choose the Right Power Bank Manufacturer?

1. Define Your Requirements First

Before you contact suppliers, know your target capacity (mAh), output power (W), battery type, certs needed, and target price.

"Sarah spent three months talking to a factory that claimed 20,000mAh capacity. After samples came, tests showed only 14,200mAh — a 29% gap. She switched to a maker with verified test reports and launched on time."

2. Verify Factory Credentials

Ask for ISO 9001 certs, CE/FCC test reports, and UN38.3 battery safety papers. Real factories should give these without delay.

3. Check MOQ Alignment

Factory MOQ varies a lot — from 500 units (WOWOHCOOL, Allway) to 10,000+ (Anker). Pick a supplier whose MOQ fits your order size. Don't over-commit to high MOQs as a startup.

4. Request Samples

Always order samples before bulk orders. A reliable maker will send samples in 3-7 days. Test charging speed, build quality, battery life, and cert claims.

5. Evaluate R&D Capability

For custom ODM projects, make sure the maker has in-house engineers. Ask about their PCB design, tooling, and heat management skills.

5. Which Manufacturing Hub Is Best for Power Banks?

Your choice of city impacts cost, speed, and quality. Here is how the three main hubs compare:

Shenzhen

The biggest electronics hub in the world. Shenzhen offers the fastest prototyping (48-hour PCB), the widest parts access, and the most skilled R&D engineers. Labor costs are highest here but still competitive globally.

Best for: Custom OEM projects, fast prototyping, premium products, complex designs. WOWOHCOOL is based here — with 24-hour engineering support and same-day part sourcing.

Dongguan

Home to massive factories with lower land and labor costs. Dongguan is the manufacturing backbone of the Pearl River Delta. It has hundreds of mid-to-large battery and power bank factories with a strong PCB and molding supply chain.

Best for: High-volume runs, cost-sensitive projects, established designs. Good when you need scale without Shenzhen prices.

Guangzhou

A growing hub with more battery and accessory factories. Guangzhou offers pricing between Shenzhen and Dongguan, with good port access for export. The Canton Fair (held here twice a year) is a natural meeting point for global buyers.

Best for: Mid-volume production, export ease, Canton Fair sourcing. Good for brands that value logistics speed.

Insider Tip: Many Shenzhen makers (including WOWOHCOOL) have extra lines in Dongguan for big orders. This gives you Shenzhen R&D speed with Dongguan's low costs at scale.

6. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average MOQ for power bank factories in China?

MOQ typically ranges from 500 to 10,000 units depending on whether you choose OEM or ODM. WOWOHCOOL offers flexible MOQ starting at 500 units for OEM projects.

How much does it cost to manufacture a custom power bank?

B2B wholesale pricing depends on capacity, battery type, and order volume. Typical FOB Shenzhen pricing ranges from $5-$25 per unit for 10,000mAh-40,000mAh power banks.

What certifications are required for selling power banks in the US and EU?

US requires FCC and UL/ETL; EU requires CE and RoHS. For air shipping, UN38.3 battery certification is mandatory. Always verify your manufacturer provides these.
