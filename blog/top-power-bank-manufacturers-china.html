How to Choose the Right Power Bank Manufacturer

1. Define Your Requirements First

Before contacting suppliers, know your target capacity (mAh), output power (W), battery type (Li-polymer vs. semi-solid-state), certifications needed, and target price point.

"When Sarah launched her outdoor gear brand, she spent three months negotiating with a factory that claimed 20,000mAh capacity. After samples arrived, independent testing showed only 14,200mAh — a 29% deficit. She switched to a manufacturer with verified test reports and launched on schedule."

2. Verify Factory Credentials

Request ISO 9001 certification, CE/FCC test reports, and UN38.3 battery safety certificates. Legitimate manufacturers should provide these without hesitation.

3. Check MOQ Alignment

Factory MOQ varies significantly — from 500 units (WOWOHCOOL, Allway) to 10,000+ (Anker). Choose a supplier whose MOQ matches your order volume. Don't over-commit to high MOQs as a startup.

4. Request Samples

Always order samples before placing bulk orders. A reliable manufacturer will offer samples within 3-7 days. Test charging speed, build quality, battery cycle life, and certification compliance.

5. Evaluate R&D Capability

For custom ODM projects, ensure the manufacturer has in-house engineering. Ask about their PCB design, tooling, and thermal management expertise.

5. Regional Manufacturing Hubs: Where to Source

Your choice of manufacturing city impacts cost, speed, and quality. Here's how the three main hubs compare for power bank production:

Shenzhen

The largest electronics ecosystem in the world. Shenzhen offers the fastest prototyping (48-hour PCB turnaround), the widest component availability, and the most experienced R&D engineers. Labor costs are highest among the three hubs but still competitive globally.

Best for: Custom OEM projects, fast prototyping, premium products, complex designs. WOWOHCOOL is based here — advantage of 24-hour engineering support and same-day component sourcing.

Dongguan

Hosts massive-scale factories with lower land and labor costs. Dongguan is the manufacturing backbone of the Pearl River Delta, home to hundreds of mid-to-large capacity battery and power bank factories. Established PCB and injection molding supply chain.

Best for: High-volume production runs, cost-sensitive projects, established product designs. Good choice when you need scale without Shenzhen's premium pricing.

Guangzhou

Emerging manufacturing hub with a growing concentration of battery and power accessory factories. Guangzhou offers competitive pricing between Shenzhen and Dongguan, with good port access for export shipments. The Canton Fair (held here biannually) makes it a natural meeting point for international buyers.

Best for: Mid-volume production, import/export convenience, Canton Fair sourcing. Suitable for brands that value logistics efficiency.

Insider Tip: Many Shenzhen-based manufacturers (including WOWOHCOOL) have satellite production lines in Dongguan for high-volume orders. This gives you the best of both worlds — Shenzhen R&D and prototyping speed with Dongguan's cost efficiency at scale.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average MOQ for power bank factories in China?

MOQ typically ranges from 500 to 10,000 units depending on whether you choose OEM or ODM. WOWOHCOOL offers flexible MOQ starting at 500 units for OEM projects.

How much does it cost to manufacture a custom power bank?

B2B wholesale pricing depends on capacity, battery type, and order volume. Typical FOB Shenzhen pricing ranges from $5-$25 per unit for 10,000mAh-40,000mAh power banks.

What certifications are required for selling power banks in the US and EU?

US requires FCC and UL/ETL; EU requires CE and RoHS. For air shipping, UN38.3 battery certification is mandatory. Always verify your manufacturer provides these.
