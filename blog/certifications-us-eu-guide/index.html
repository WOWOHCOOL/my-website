1. North American Market: Safety & Interference

UL / ETL (62368-1)

The gold standard for North America. UL 62368-1 covers audio and IT gear. WOWOHCOOL uses V0 fire-retardant materials that exceed UL 94. This ensures our chargers pass the strictest safety tests.

FCC (Part 15)

Ensures your chargers don't interfere with other electronics or emergency services. Covers unintentional radiators like USB chargers. Both SDoC and cert routes are accepted.

DOE / CEC (Level VI)

Mandatory energy rules for the US. Level VI needs average efficiency above 87% for small power supplies. Critical for Amazon and major stores. Non-compliant products face import limits and marketplace removal.

Energy Star

Voluntary but often needed by big stores. Energy Star shows premium efficiency to buyers. It helps your product stand out on Amazon listing pages. WOWOHCOOL offers Energy Star designs across many charger types.

California Prop 65

Chemical exposure warnings for products sold in California. Affects all chargers with solder, cables, or plastics. Enforcement through private litigation——costly if missed.

2. What EU Certifications Do Chargers Need?

CE Marking (LVD & EMC)

The mandatory "passport" for the 27 EU nations. Without a valid CE mark, you cannot sell products in any EU state. For chargers, CE marking requires:

  • LVD 2014/35/EU: Electrical safety below 1000V AC
  • EMC 2014/30/EU: Electromagnetic compatibility
  • RED 2014/53/EU: Radio equipment (for wireless chargers)

RoHS & REACH

Rules for hazardous substances. RoHS bans 10 materials like lead, mercury, and phthalates. REACH tracks SVHC chemicals above 0.1%.

ErP Directive

High efficiency and low standby power below 0.3W. ErP Tier 2 needs VI level (average efficiency above 88%). Important for energy-conscious European buyers.

UK Post-Brexit: UKCA Marking

Post-Brexit, the UK needs UKCA marking instead of CE. CE is still accepted during a transition until 2027. WOWOHCOOL supports both marks for seamless UK and EU sales.

3. Essential Comparison: US vs. EU Requirements

The US and EU have different certification rules. Know these differences to plan your market entry. Both regions care about electrical safety and EMC. But their specific rules, test steps, and paperwork differ a lot. The table below shows the key requirements:

Region Primary Safety Efficiency Standard Environmental
USA UL 62368-1 / ETL DOE Level VI Prop 65
Europe CE (EN 62368-1) ERP Stage 2 RoHS / REACH / WEEE
Canada cUL / CSA NRCan RoHS equivalent
UK UKCA / BS EN 62368-1 ERP Stage 2 RoHS / WEEE

6. Certification Checklist: Before You Ship

Before your first shipment, gather these documents. This checklist helps you avoid costly customs delays. Requirements may vary by product type. Wireless chargers need extra RED testing for EU markets. Power banks need UN38.3 battery certification too. Always check the latest rules with your certification partner before shipping.

US Market Checklist

  • UL or ETL listing certificate (valid, unexpired)
  • FCC SDoC or certification number
  • Prop 65 test report or supplier declarations
  • UN38.3 test summary for lithium batteries
  • Product labels with FCC ID and UL mark

EU Market Checklist

  • CE Declaration of Conformity (signed)
  • Test reports from accredited laboratory
  • RoHS test report or supplier declarations
  • Technical file (TF) documentation
  • Product with CE mark and WEEE symbol

8. Common Certification Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Even experienced importers make mistakes. These errors cost thousands in delays and lost sales. The best brands work with factories that have in-house compliance teams. These experts know both the rules and the manufacturing process. Learn from these common pitfalls to save your next product launch.

Relying on a Single PDF Certificate

A PDF can be forged. Always check cert numbers on the issuer's database (UL iQ, TUV portal, IECEE CB). Real certs have unique, checkable numbers. WOWOHCOOL gives full traceability with verifiable cert numbers for every product.

Assuming One Certification Covers All Markets

CE does not work in the US. UL does not work in the EU. Each market has its own rules. Assuming cross-recognition leads to customs seizures. The CB Scheme cuts redundant tests. But you still need separate market certs.

Ignoring Manufacturing Changes After Certification

Changing a part, PCB layout, or factory can void your cert. Big changes need re-testing. WOWOHCOOL tracks every production change and tells clients when re-cert may be needed.

Forgetting Certificate Renewal Dates

Certs expire. UL needs yearly factory audits. FCC has renewal cycles. An expired cert means your product is not compliant. WOWOHCOOL's team tracks renewal dates and handles re-cert for all active product lines.

Cost-Saving Strategies

10. Future Regulatory Trends to Watch

Watch for new rules that will affect charger imports. Trends point to stricter environmental standards, more supply chain transparency, and digital documentation. Plan for these changes now to avoid costly fixes later.

EU USB-C Mandate (2026)

From December 2026, new EU rules require USB-C ports on all portable devices. This covers power banks, wireless chargers, and wall adapters. Make sure your designs meet EN/IEC 62680-1-3 for USB-C. WOWOHCOOL chargers already comply. We help clients update their designs before the deadline.

According to the European Commission, the Common Charger Directive mandates USB-C across all EU member states, directly impacting charger import compliance.

EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products

New ESPR rules will ask for digital product passports and repairability scores. Chargers must meet Tier 3 efficiency by 2027. Standby power must go below 0.1W. Plan your product roadmap now.

US Supply Chain Traceability

The UFLPA act asks importers to show supply chain records. Keep records of parts, factory audits, and batch tracking. WOWOHCOOL keeps full supply chain records for all export orders.
