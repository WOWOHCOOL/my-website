1. Qi2 and WPC Membership Verification

Why is WPC (Wireless Power Consortium) membership critical? Without proper membership, factories cannot legally use the Qi logo, exposing your brand to legal risks and product recalls.

What to Verify

  • • WPC Member Status: Ask for their official membership number and verify at wpc.com
  • • Qi2 Certification: Ensure they have MPP (Magnetic Power Profile) certified modules
  • • Test Lab Credentials: Verify they use authorized testing facilities (ATL)

Learn more: Qi2 vs MagSafe Guide →

2. FOD (Foreign Object Detection) Testing

Core Pain Point: If users place coins or metal objects on the charger, will it overheat or catch fire? This is the #1 cause of wireless charger returns.

Expert Recommendation

Request the factory to demonstrate their FOD algorithm sensitivity. Quality factories should detect metal objects within 2mm of the charging surface.

WOWOHCOOL Solution

Our wireless charger solutions use NP0 capacitors to ensure stable FOD detection even under high-temperature conditions. This premium component prevents false triggers and ensures safety compliance.

4. Thermal Management and Shell Materials

Wireless charging generates heat—the key is how the factory manages it. Poor thermal design leads to slower charging and safety hazards.

Thermal Pad

Check if factory uses thermal interface materials (thermal pads/silicone) between coil and shell to transfer heat away from the phone.

Aluminum Base

Premium factories use aluminum alloy base plates for superior heat dissipation compared to plastic-only designs.

5. PCBA Surface SMT Quality

Clean SMT production lines determine PCBA failure rates. Request to see their workshop photos and reflow oven specifications.

Expert Tip

If a factory quotes you a Qi2 price without a real MPP module, your product will be limited to 5W on iPhones. Always ask for the module source and verify it has MPP certification.

3. How to Verify a Legitimate Factory

Before committing to any supplier, verify their legitimacy through these steps:

1

Check Business Registration

Verify the factory's business license. A legitimate manufacturing facility will have import/export license, ISO 9001 certification capability, and a physical factory address.

2

Request Factory Documentation

Ask for factory photos (including production lines), employee count verification, business license copy, and ISO certification if claimed.

3

Verify Contact Information

Legitimate factories have direct phone numbers, company email domains (not Gmail/Yahoo), and respond promptly during business hours.

4

Check Trade Records

Request export data or check Alibaba trade records. Real factories have documented export history.

Red Flag

If they only send product catalogs without factory documentation, you're likely dealing with a trading company, not a manufacturer. Check our sourcing guides for more tips.

7. Red Flags to Watch Out For

Watch for these warning signs when evaluating factories:

Unrealistically Low Prices

If the price seems too good to be true, it probably is. Quality has a cost.

Refuses to Provide Documentation

Legitimate factories readily provide business licenses, certifications, and factory photos.

Poor Communication

Slow responses or vague answers often indicate reliability issues.

No Sample Policy

Refusing to provide samples before bulk orders is a major red flag.

Can't Verify Certifications

Always verify certificates with issuing bodies. Fake certifications are common.

9. Sample Evaluation Process

Before committing to bulk orders, evaluate samples systematically. This sample evaluation process helps you make data-driven decisions:

Sample Evaluation Timeline

Day 1-3
Request Multiple Samples

Request samples from 3-5 factories simultaneously. Specify exact requirements including charging speed, materials, and certifications needed.

Day 4-10
Physical Inspection

Check build quality, finish, weight, and alignment. Test all charging positions and verify LED indicators function correctly.

Day 11-14
Performance Testing

Test charging efficiency using professional equipment. Check heat generation during extended charging sessions and verify Qi compatibility.

Day 15-21
Certification Verification

Verify certificates directly with issuing bodies. Request test reports and cross-reference with factory claims.

Sample Evaluation Criteria

Appearance (20%)
  • • Surface finish quality
  • • Color accuracy
  • • Logo print durability
  • • Packaging integrity
Functionality (50%)
  • • Charging speed
  • • Compatibility
  • • Heat management
  • • Safety features
Documentation (30%)
  • • Test reports
  • • Certificates
  • • Compliance docs
  • • Manuals accuracy

Pro Tip

Request 10-20 samples for extended testing. This allows you to identify consistency issues across batches—a critical indicator of production quality control.

10. Communication & Language Considerations

Effective communication determines the success of your factory relationship. Here's how to navigate language barriers:

Recommended Communication Channels

Primary Channels
  • WeChat - Quick updates, images
  • Email - Formal documentation
  • WhatsApp - International clients
  • Phone - Urgent matters
Response Time Expectations
  • WeChat: 2-4 hours
  • Email: 24 hours
  • Phone: Immediate

Language Best Practices

  • Use clear, simple English - Avoid idioms, slang, and complex technical jargon
  • Provide visual references - Include diagrams, photos, and videos when explaining requirements
  • Confirm understanding - Request written confirmation for critical specifications
  • Document everything - Keep written records of all verbal agreements
  • Use translation tools wisely - Google Translate can help but verify critical details manually

Trust Building

Key Elements of a Successful Partnership

1
Clear Specifications

Provide detailed technical specifications and quality standards in writing. This prevents misunderstandings and ensures consistency.

2
Regular Feedback

Share market feedback with your factory. This helps them improve products and gives you early access to upgrades.

When issues arise (and they will), work together on solutions. This builds trust and prevents future problems.
