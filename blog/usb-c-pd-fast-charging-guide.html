1. What is USB-C PD 3.1 and PPS? (The Technical Edge)

USB-C Power Delivery (PD) is a fast charging standard developed by the USB Implementers Forum (USB-IF). PD 3.1, the latest iteration, delivers up to 240W of power—enough for gaming laptops, workstations, and even desktop equipment. PPS (Programmable Power Supply) enables fine-grained voltage control in 20mV increments, reducing heat generation by up to 30%.

Key PD 3.1 Advantages

  • 240W EPR - Extended Power Range for workstations
  • PPS Technology - 30% less heat generation
  • Universal compatibility across all USB-C devices
  • Bi-directional power - Device to device charging

Factory Integration

Our latest GaN Chargers utilize PPS (Programmable Power Supply) to dynamically adjust voltage, significantly reducing heat—a critical factor for high-power desktop stations. USB-IF certification ensures compatibility.

Why PPS Matters: Samsung's 45W Super Fast Charging 2.0 requires PPS to work properly. Without PPS, high-wattage Samsung devices default to standard charging speeds. Every WOWOHCOOL charger with 45W+ output includes PPS support.

4. Safety & The E-Marker Chip Factor

Professional buyers understand that high-wattage PD requires more than just a powerful charger. Safety mechanisms are non-negotiable.

The 60W Cable Limit

USB-C cables without E-Marker chips are limited to 3A (60W). For anything above 60W, you must use cables with certified E-Marker chips. Using an under-rated cable with a 100W charger is a fire hazard.

  • 60W maximum: Standard USB-C cable (3A, no E-Marker)
  • 100W maximum: 5A cable with E-Marker required
  • 240W maximum: EPR cable with E-Marker required
