How to Calculate Import Costs from China

Learn how to accurately calculate import costs from China including product price, shipping, customs duties, taxes, and hidden fees.

1. Understanding Landed Cost

When importing from China, the unit price is just the beginning. True landed cost includes all expenses from factory to your warehouse. Understanding this helps you price competitively and avoid unexpected losses.

Landed Cost Formula

Landed Cost = Product Price + Shipping + Insurance + Customs Duties + Taxes + Handling Fees

Understanding Incoterms 2020 is essential for knowing who pays which costs. Common terms include FOB (Free on Board), CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight), and DDP (Delivered Duty Paid).

2. Product Pricing Components

Factory pricing depends on several factors. Here's what affects your per-unit cost:

Order Quantity

MOQs typically range from 500-1000 units. Larger orders = lower unit price. Expect 5-15% discount for 5,000+ units.

Customization Level

Logo printing, custom packaging, and color matching add $0.20-$2.00 per unit depending on complexity.

Certifications Required

Qi, CE, FCC, RoHS certifications add $0.50-$3.00 per unit. Pre-certified modules reduce costs.

Payment Terms

TT in advance offers 2-5% discount vs. LC at sight. 30% deposit / 70% before shipping is common.

3. International Shipping Costs

Shipping costs vary significantly based on method, volume, and destination. Here's a comparison:

Method Transit Time Cost (per kg) Best For
Express (DHL/FedEx) 3-5 days $5-8/kg Samples, small orders
Air Freight 7-12 days $3-5/kg 100-500 kg shipments
Sea Freight 25-35 days $0.50-1.5/kg Full container loads

Pro Tip

For wireless chargers (avg 150g/unit), sea freight adds only $0.08-0.15 per unit vs. $0.75-1.20 for air. Worth the 25-day wait for large orders.

4. Customs Duties and Taxes

Import duties vary by country and product category. Here's what to expect:

USA (HS Code 8517.62)

  • • Import Duty: 0-6.5% (most chargers 0%)
  • • Section 301 Tariffs: 0-25% (if applicable)
  • • Merchandise Processing Fee: 0.3464%
  • • Harbor Maintenance Fee: 0.125%

European Union

  • • Import Duty: 0% (electronics under 8517)
  • • VAT: 19-27% (country dependent)
  • • Customs clearance: €15-30

HS Code Reference

Wireless chargers typically fall under HS Code 8517.62 (machines for reception of voice/images). Always verify with your freight forwarder before shipping.

5. Hidden Costs to Consider

Inspection Costs

Third-party inspection (SGS, Bureau Veritas): $250-500 per inspection. Recommended for orders over $10,000.

Insurance

Marine cargo insurance: 0.1-0.3% of CIF value. Essential for valuable shipments.

Warehouse & Handling

Destination port handling: $50-150. Customs brokerage: $100-300 if using a broker.

Return/Defect Costs

Include 2-5% buffer for potential defects. Shipping returns from USA to China: $8-15/unit via sea.

6. Calculation Example

Let's calculate the landed cost for 5,000 wireless chargers shipped from China to USA:

Example: 5,000 Wireless Chargers

Product Price (FOB) $12.00 × 5,000 = $60,000
Sea Freight (750kg, $1/kg) $750
Insurance (0.2%) $121.50
Customs Duty (0%) $0
MPF (0.3464%) $207.84
Brokerage & Handling $250
TOTAL LANDED COST $61,329.34
Cost Per Unit $12.27

Key Takeaway

Adding just $0.27 per unit to your product cost covers all shipping and import fees. Always factor this into your pricing strategy to maintain profit margins.
