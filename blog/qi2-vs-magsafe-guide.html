1. What is Qi Wireless Charging?

Qi (pronounced "chee") is the world's most widely adopted wireless charging standard, developed by the Wireless Power Consortium (WPC). Since its launch in 2008, Qi has become the universal standard for inductive wireless charging, with over 1 billion devices in active use globally.

The Qi standard operates through electromagnetic induction between two coils: a transmitter coil in the charging pad and a receiver coil in the device. When aligned properly, the transmitter generates an alternating electromagnetic field that induces an electric current in the receiver coil, which then charges the device's battery.

Qi Standard Key Facts

  • 2008 - Qi standard launched by WPC
  • 1 billion+ Qi-enabled devices in circulation
  • 500+ certified products worldwide
  • 15W maximum power (Baseline Power Profile)
  • 60-70% charging efficiency rate
  • 350+ member companies in the WPC

Qi Advantages

  • Universal compatibility across all brands and devices
  • Massive ecosystem of accessories and charging pads
  • Lower cost products available from multiple manufacturers
  • Proven, battle-tested technology with 18+ years of refinement
  • Backward compatible with older Qi devices
  • Supported by major retailers and public charging stations

Qi Limitations

  • No magnetic alignment - requires manual positioning
  • Lower efficiency compared to Qi2 and MagSafe
  • Heat generation reduces long-term battery health
  • Slow charging speeds (5-15W) for most devices
  • Foreign object detection (FOD) varies by manufacturer

2. What is MagSafe?

MagSafe is Apple's proprietary wireless charging system, introduced in October 2020 alongside the iPhone 12 series. Named after the magnetic MacBook power connector, MagSafe uses an array of magnets to align the charger perfectly with the device, enabling faster and more efficient charging than standard Qi.

The MagSafe ecosystem includes theofficial Apple MagSafe Charger (15W), MagSafe Duo Charger, and MagSafe Battery Pack. Third-party manufacturers can create MagSafe-compatible accessories through Apple's Made for MagSafe (MFM) program, though certification requirements are strict and licensing fees apply.

MagSafe Key Facts

  • 2020 - Introduced with iPhone 12 series
  • 15W maximum charging speed (iPhone 12-16)
  • 75-80% charging efficiency rate
  • 12+ mm alignment magnet ring
  • MFi/MFM certification required for accessories
  • Apple-exclusive - limited to iOS devices

MagSafe Advantages

  • Perfect magnetic alignment every time
  • Faster 15W charging vs standard Qi (7.5W for iPhone)
  • Snap-on ecosystem of premium accessories
  • Premium build quality and user experience
  • Consistent performance across Apple devices
  • Compatible with MagSafe cases and mounts

MagSafe Limitations

  • Apple-exclusive - no Android support
  • Premium pricing ($30-80 for official chargers)
  • MFi/MFM certification adds 15-20% to product costs
  • Limited third-party ecosystem (vs Qi)
  • Not truly universal across device brands
  • Heat management challenges at sustained 15W

3. What is Qi2?

Qi2 (Qi v2.0) is the newest wireless charging standard, officially launched by the WPC in January 2023. It represents a paradigm shift in wireless charging by combining the universal compatibility of Qi with the magnetic alignment pioneered by MagSafe.

Qi2 introduces Magnetically Protected Power (MPP) technology, which standardizes magnetic alignment for all manufacturers. This means any Qi2 charger will magnetically attach to any Qi2-enabled device - just like MagSafe, but open to all brands. The standard was developed with input from Apple, which contributed its MagSafe technology to the WPC for the MPP profile.

Qi2 Key Facts

  • 2023 - Qi2 standard officially launched at CES
  • 15W/25W/50W power levels (EPP profiles)
  • 80-85% charging efficiency rate
  • MPP (Magnetically Protected Power) alignment
  • Open standard - available to all WPC members
  • 200+ Qi2 certified products as of 2026

Qi2 Advantages

  • Universal magnetic alignment - works with all Qi2 devices
  • Higher power options (25W, 50W Extended Power Profile)
  • Open standard - no licensing fees like MFi/MFM
  • Better efficiency than Qi (80-85% vs 60-70%)
  • Future-proof with 100W profiles planned for 2027
  • Cross-brand compatibility unlike MagSafe

4. Qi2 Extended Power Profile (EPP) Explained

The Qi2 standard introduces Extended Power Profile (EPP) as a significant upgrade over the Baseline Power Profile (BPP) used in original Qi. EPP enables higher power transfer with improved efficiency and thermal management.

Power Transfer Range 5mm - 8mm coil distance

The EPP specification includes enhanced Foreign Object Detection (FOD) capabilities, which is critical for safety when charging at higher power levels. At 25W+ charging, even small metal objects like coins or keys on the charging pad can overheat - EPP addresses this with sub-700mW detection thresholds.

8. Which Standard Should You Choose?

Q

Choose Qi If...

  • Budget is the primary constraint
  • Maximum device compatibility is needed
  • Building a B2B accessory line for retail
  • Older device models (pre-2023) are your target
  • Public charging infrastructure is the use case
M

Choose MagSafe If...

  • Apple ecosystem is your exclusive market
  • Premium branding requires Apple's badge
  • MFi certification is already in place
  • Targeting iPhone 12-16 users specifically
  • Accessory ecosystem integration matters
Q2

Choose Qi2 If...

  • Future-proofing your product line
  • Serving both Apple and Android markets
  • Higher power delivery (25W+) is needed
  • Reducing return rates through better alignment
  • Open standard without MFi licensing costs

Recommendation Summary

For 2026 and beyond: Qi2 is the recommended standard for new product development. It offers the best balance of power (15-50W), efficiency (80-85%), universal compatibility, and open-access certification. The Qi2 ecosystem is growing rapidly, with over 200 certified products and adoption by Apple, Samsung, Google, and Xiaomi in their latest flagship devices.

9. Buyer Checklist: Choosing Wireless Chargers

Use this checklist when evaluating wireless chargers for personal use or B2B procurement. Our quality team uses this same framework when manufacturing OEM products.

1 Certification Verification

  • Qi/Qi2 certified (verify at wpc.org)
  • CE marking for EU sales
  • FCC certification for US market
  • RoHS compliance documentation

2 Technical Specifications

  • Output power matches your device (15W/25W/50W)
  • Input: USB-C PD 3.0 or QC 4+ required
  • FOD (Foreign Object Detection) included
  • Over-temperature protection
  • Over-voltage and over-current protection
  • Qi2 MPP or EPP profile verified

3 Build Quality

  • Aluminum or ABS+TPU housing (not cheap plastic)
  • Non-slip base (silicone or textured)
  • LED indicator (not too bright for bedroom use)
  • Cable length: 1.0-1.5m optimal
  • Braided or reinforced cable connectors
  • Weight: >150g for stability

4 Business Requirements (B2B)

  • MOQ fits your inventory capacity
  • Custom logo/brand packaging available
  • Sample order policy before bulk
  • Warranty: minimum 12 months
  • Defect rate guarantee in writing
  • Drop shipping fulfillment option
