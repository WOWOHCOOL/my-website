2. What Are Incoterms and Why Do They Matter?

Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) define who is responsible for what during the shipping process. Understanding these terms is essential for accurate cost calculation and risk management.

Incoterm Name Risk Transfer Best For
EXW Ex Works Seller's premises Experienced importers with own logistics
FOB Free on Board Port of loading Most common for sea freight
CIF Cost Insurance Freight Port of loading Buyer wants insurance included
DDP Delivered Duty Paid Buyer's premises Turnkey solution, higher price
DAP Delivered at Place Named place Buyer pays duties separately

Recommended Incoterms for Chargers

FOB Shenzhen / FOB Guangzhou: Most common and recommended for most importers. The seller handles factory preparation and port handling; you arrange main carriage and insurance.

DDP (Delivered Duty Paid): Best for new importers who want a single invoice covering everything. The seller handles all logistics including duties.

EXW: Only recommended if you have established relationships with freight forwarders and can arrange all logistics efficiently.

Choosing the right shipping method depends on your order volume, urgency, and budget. Here's a detailed comparison:

Sea Freight (20GP / 40GP / 40HC)

Transit Times

  • —China to US East Coast: 25-35 days
  • —China to Europe: 25-35 days
  • —China to Australia: 15-25 days

  • —0GP: ~55-58 CBM (ideal for 4,000-6,000 chargers)

  • —CAF (Currency Adjustment): 5-15% of freight rate

  • —China to Australia: 3-5 days

4. Calculating Total Landed Cost

Total Landed Cost (TLC) is the complete cost of getting your products to your warehouse, including all fees and duties. Here's how to calculate it:

Landed Cost Formula

TLC = Product Cost + Freight + Insurance + Customs Duties + Port Fees + Last Mile Delivery

  • Product Cost: FOB price from supplier
  • Freight: Sea freight ($0.05-0.15/unit) or air freight ($0.30-0.80/unit)
  • Insurance: Typically 0.2-0.5% of cargo value (CIF)
  • Customs Duties: HTS code rate × CIF value (US: 8-10% for chargers)
  • Port Fees: Terminal handling, port congestion surcharges
  • Last Mile: Trucking to your warehouse

Example Calculation: 5,000 Wireless Chargers

Product Cost (FOB) $15,000
Sea Freight (40GP) $3,000 ($0.60/unit)
Insurance (0.3%) $54
CIF Value $18,054
Customs Duty (9%) $1,625
Port Handling & Fees $350
Last Mile Delivery $400
TOTAL LANDED COST $20,429
Cost Per Unit $4.09 (vs. $3.00 FOB)

Key Insight: Shipping and duties added 36% to the product cost. Always factor this into your pricing strategy!

5. Customs Clearance Process

Customs clearance is often the most confusing part of importing. Understanding the process helps avoid delays and unexpected costs.

2026 Lithium Battery Shipping Update

Power banks and devices with built-in lithium batteries (UN3481) face new shipping regulations effective January 1, 2026. The IMDG Code 42-24 and IATA DGR 67th Edition now require:

  • SOC �?30% (State of Charge) for both air and sea shipments
  • Valid UN38.3 test report from CNAS/CMA accredited lab
  • MSDS must state SOC �?30% compliance
  • Lithium battery mark (100×100mm) on outer packaging
  • For EU destinations: battery passport traceability required

A good freight forwarder can save you money and headaches. Here's what to look for:

7. Shipping Tips for Chargers

Optimize Packaging

Standard retail packaging can be compressed. Individual boxes nested together in cartons maximize container space. For chargers, a 40GP holds approximately 8,000-12,000 units with standard retail packaging.

Consolidate Shipments

If ordering from multiple suppliers, consolidate shipments into one container. LCL (Less than Container Load) consolidation saves money but adds handling time and risk.

Book During Off-Peak

Shipping rates spike during September-November (pre-holiday) and Chinese New Year. Book containers 4-6 weeks in advance during peak season to secure space and rates.

Insure Your Cargo

Marine cargo insurance costs 0.1-0.5% of cargo value but protects against loss, damage, and delays. Standard coverage excludes rough handling and moisture damage——get all-risk coverage for peace of mind.

Plan for Port Congestion

Major ports experience delays during peak seasons. Los Angeles/Long Beach currently averages 3-7 days of waiting time. Build this buffer into your inventory planning or consider alternative ports.

Track Your Shipment

Most freight forwarders provide online tracking for sea and air shipments. Request tracking credentials and monitor your shipment's progress. For high-value orders, consider real-time GPS trackers that can be placed inside containers for complete visibility throughout the journey.

Battery Shipping Compliance (UN38.3)

Power banks and chargers with lithium batteries require UN38.3 certification for air transport. This applies to both sea and air freight. Without proper UN38.3 test reports, airlines will reject your cargo.
