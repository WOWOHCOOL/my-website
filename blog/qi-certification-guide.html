1. What is Qi Certification?

Qi (pronounced "chee") is the global standard for wireless power transfer using inductive charging technology. Developed and maintained by the Wireless Power Consortium (WPC), Qi certification ensures that wireless charging devices work safely and efficiently with all other Qi-enabled products worldwide.

The Qi standard has evolved significantly since its introduction in 2008. Today, it powers over one billion devices globally, from smartphones and earbuds to laptop computers and automotive applications. The Qi specification defines everything from power transfer efficiency to foreign object detection protocols.

Qi certification is not optional for manufacturers who want to use the Qi logo. It's a rigorous process that validates your product meets all safety, interoperability, and performance requirements defined by the WPC. Without proper Qi certification, you cannot legally use the Qi logo on your products or packaging.

2. Why Qi Certification Matters

Qi certification is essential for any wireless charger entering the global market. Here's why:

Safety Guaranteed

Certified products undergo rigorous safety testing including foreign object detection (FOD), temperature management, and electromagnetic compatibility. Wireless chargers that fail these tests can overheat, damage devices, or even cause fires. Qi certification protects your customers and your brand reputation.

Interoperability

Qi certification ensures your charger works seamlessly with all Qi-enabled devices from any manufacturer - Samsung, Apple, Google, Sony, and hundreds of other brands. Your customers expect their new charger to work with their existing devices, and Qi certification guarantees this compatibility.

Retail Requirements

Major retailers including Best Buy, Amazon, Walmart, Target, and Currys require Qi certification for shelf placement. Many e-commerce platforms will remove non-certified wireless charger listings. Without certification, you're locked out of the largest sales channels.

Legal Compliance

Using the Qi logo without certification is trademark infringement and can result in legal action, product recalls, and significant financial penalties. The WPC actively monitors the market for unauthorized Qi logo usage and pursues enforcement actions against violators.

Consumer Confidence

Today's informed consumers actively look for the Qi logo when purchasing wireless chargers. The Qi mark has become synonymous with safe, reliable wireless charging. Displaying the Qi logo on your products builds trust and justifies premium pricing.

3. Qi Certification Process Step-by-Step

The Qi certification process involves several critical steps that typically span 8-16 weeks. Working with an experienced OEM/ODM partner can significantly streamline this journey.

1

Join the WPC

Register as a WPC member at wirelesspowerconsortium.com. Annual fees range from $2,500 (small company) to $15,000+ (large manufacturer). Your membership level determines your access to working group activities and early specification updates.

Duration: 1-2 weeks for application approval

2

Product Registration

Submit your product details through the WPC member portal, including design specifications, intended Qi power classes (5W, 7.5W, 10W, 15W, or the newer Extended Power Profile up to 65W), coil configurations, and intended receiver device compatibility.

Duration: 1-3 days

3

Sample Preparation

Prepare production-representative samples for testing. These samples must match your final production units exactly - same components, same PCB layout, same firmware. Any differences between test samples and production units will invalidate your certification.

Duration: 2-6 weeks depending on product complexity

4

Authorized Laboratory Testing

Send samples to an authorized Qi testing laboratory. These facilities are accredited by the WPC to perform compliance testing. Testing covers power transfer efficiency, foreign object detection, temperature management, EMF emissions, and interoperability with reference receivers. Browse our Qi-certified wireless charger collection to see products that have passed these rigorous tests.

Duration: 2-6 weeks depending on test complexity and lab scheduling

5

Certification Review

WPC reviews test results and supporting documentation. If all requirements are met, your product receives Qi certification and is listed in the official Qi certified product database.

Duration: 1-2 weeks

6

Logo Activation

Once certified, you receive the official Qi logo files and guidelines for proper usage. You can now legally use the Qi logo on your products, packaging, and marketing materials.

4. Detailed Testing Requirements

Qi testing is comprehensive and covers multiple aspects of wireless charging technology. All tests are conducted according to the latest Qi specification version.

Test Category Key Requirements Failure Impact
Power Transfer Efficiency Must maintain ≥70% efficiency at rated power levels across all operating conditions Excessive heat generation, device damage, failed certification
Foreign Object Detection (FOD) Must detect and respond to metal objects (coins, keys) within specified time limits; varies by power class Fire risk, safety hazard, product recall
Temperature Control Surface temperature must stay below 40°C under all charging conditions; coil temperature limits apply Safety hazard, device damage, failed certification
EMF Emissions Must comply with ICNIRP guidelines for human exposure to electromagnetic fields Regulatory violation, market access denial
Interoperability Testing Must work correctly with reference Qi receivers and at least three different certified transmitter devices Failed certification, interoperability issues
Power Class Compliance Must meet all requirements specific to declared power class (BPP, EPP, MPP) Certification denial, re-testing required
Coil Alignment Must maintain stable power transfer within specified alignment tolerances Intermittent charging, user complaints

Testing Tip

Pre-compliance testing at independent labs before official WPC testing can save thousands of dollars. Identify and fix issues early rather than failing official tests and paying for re-testing.

7. Qi2 vs Qi Certification Differences

The introduction of Qi2 in 2023 marked a significant evolution in wireless charging technology. Understanding the differences between Qi (now called Qi v1.x or BPP/EPP) and Qi2 is essential for product planning.

Feature Qi (BPP/EPP) Qi2 (MPP)
Magnetic Alignment No (relies on free positioning) Yes (MagSafe-compatible ring)
Efficiency 65-75% typical 80%+ with aligned magnets
Power Output Up to 15W (EPP) Up to 15W baseline, extensible to 100W+
Apple Support iPhone 8 - iPhone 14 (7.5W) iPhone 12+ full 15W (Made for iPhone)
Android Support Samsung, Google, others (varies) Growing Android support expected
Testing Complexity Moderate Higher (includes magnetic testing)
Certification Cost $5,000-$15,000 $10,000-$20,000
Market Demand Established, declining Growing rapidly

Which Standard Should You Target?

Qi (BPP/EPP): Best for budget chargers, car mounts, furniture integration, and markets where Qi2 accessories aren't prevalent. Lower certification costs.

Qi2 (MPP): Best for premium chargers targeting iPhone 12+ users, magnetic accessories (power banks, stands), and future-proofing your product line.

Many manufacturers are now pursuing dual certification, offering products that support both Qi and Qi2 for maximum market coverage. Our Qi-certified wireless chargers include options in both categories.

8. Common Certification Mistakes to Avoid

Based on our experience helping dozens of brands through the Qi certification process, here are the most common pitfalls and how to avoid them:

Mistake #1: Sample-Production Mismatch

The most common reason for certification failure. Test samples must exactly match production units. Even minor component changes (different MOSFET, adjusted coil winding) require re-testing. Always lock your BOM before certification testing.

Mistake #2: Insufficient FOD Testing

Foreign Object Detection is one of the most commonly failed test categories. Test with a comprehensive set of metal objects (coins, paper clips, keys, metal plates) at various positions. Don't assume your FOD algorithm is robust without thorough testing.

Mistake #3: Underestimating Thermal Design

Temperature limits are strict (surface <40°C). Thermal management requires careful PCB layout, component selection, and enclosure design. Test thermal performance under worst-case conditions (high ambient temperature, maximum load).

Mistake #4: Wrong Power Class Selection

Claiming higher power capability than your product can deliver is a serious violation. Be conservative in your power class declaration. You can always add higher power modes in future certification iterations.

Mistake #5: Incomplete Documentation

WPC certification requires detailed documentation including schematics, BOM, firmware version, test setup descriptions, and user manuals. Missing or incomplete documentation delays the certification review process significantly.

Mistake #6: Ignoring Regional Requirements

Qi certification is separate from regulatory certifications (FCC, CE, RoHS). You'll need both. Plan your regulatory certification timeline to align with or precede Qi certification. See our US/EU certifications guide for details.

9. Working with China Factory for Certification

China remains the world's dominant manufacturing hub for wireless chargers. Partnering with an experienced China-based manufacturer can significantly streamline your Qi certification journey.

Benefits of China Manufacturing for Qi Certification

  • Pre-certified platforms: Many Chinese manufacturers, including WOWOHCOOL, offer products with existing Qi certification that you can white-label or customize.
  • Faster iteration: In-house prototyping, testing, and production allows rapid fixes when issues arise during certification testing.
  • Cost efficiency: Manufacturing in China reduces unit costs, offsetting some certification expenses.
  • Established lab relationships: Experienced manufacturers have working relationships with authorized testing laboratories, streamlining scheduling and communication.
  • Component sourcing: Chinese manufacturers have supply chain relationships for Qi-certified components (TX coils, controllers) that may not be readily available elsewhere.

Key Considerations When Selecting a China Partner

Verify Existing Certifications

Ask for proof of existing Qi certifications and verify them in the WPC product database. Request test reports to understand their quality history.

Understand IP Protection

Before sharing your product specifications, ensure proper NDA agreements are in place. Work with manufacturers experienced in serving international brands who understand confidentiality requirements.

Clarify Certification Ownership

Determine whether the Qi certification will be under the manufacturer's WPC membership or yours. For brand-specific certification, you'll need your own WPC membership and certification in your company name.

Plan for Mass Production Consistency

Certification is based on specific samples. Ensure the manufacturer has robust quality control processes to maintain certification compliance during mass production. Request first article inspection reports.

10. Qi Logo Usage Rules

Once certified, you must follow strict rules when using the Qi logo. The Qi logo usage guidelines are part of your WPC membership agreement

.

Use only on certified products listed in the WPC database

Display the logo exactly as provided by WPC (no modifications to colors, proportions, or design)

Include Qi logo in product packaging, user manuals, and marketing materials

The Qi logo must be clearly visible on the product itself or its retail packaging

Never use on non-certified products or accessories (including bundled cables)

Don't claim "Qi compatible" or "Works with Qi" without official certification

Don't use Qi logos in ways that suggest endorsement by WPC beyond your specific certified products
