1. What is Qi Certification?

Qi (pronounced "chee") is the global standard for wireless power. It uses inductive charging tech. The Wireless Power Consortium (WPC) runs this standard. Qi cert makes sure your chargers work safely with all Qi devices worldwide. For a detailed explanation of the underlying technology, see How Wireless Charging Works.

The Qi standard has grown a lot since 2008. Today, it powers over one billion devices worldwide. This covers smartphones, earbuds, laptops, and car chargers.

Qi cert is not optional if you want the Qi logo. It's a strict process that checks safety, cross-device work, and performance. Without proper Qi cert, you cannot legally use the Qi logo on your products or packing.

2. Why Qi Certification Matters

According to the Wireless Power Consortium (WPC), over 10,000 Qi-certified products have been registered globally.

Qi certification is essential for any wireless charger entering the global market. Here's why:

Safety Guaranteed

Certified products pass strict safety tests. These include FOD, temperature checks, and EMC testing. Failed chargers can overheat or damage devices. Qi cert protects your customers and your brand.

Interoperability

Qi cert makes sure your charger works with all Qi devices. Samsung, Apple, Google, Sony, and more. Your customers expect their new charger to work with their gear. Qi cert guarantees this cross-brand fit.

Retail Requirements

Big stores need Qi cert for shelf space. Best Buy, Amazon, Walmart, Target, and Currys. Many platforms will remove non-cert listings. Without Qi cert, you are locked out of top sales channels.

Legal Compliance

Using the Qi logo without cert is trademark theft. It can lead to lawsuits, recalls, and big fines. The WPC monitors the market for fake Qi logo use. They take action against violators.

Consumer Confidence

Today's savvy buyers look for the Qi logo when shopping. The Qi mark means safe, reliable wireless charging. Using the Qi logo on your products builds trust. It also supports premium pricing.

3. Qi Certification Process Step-by-Step

The Qi cert process has several key steps. It usually takes 8-16 weeks. Working with an experienced OEM/ODM partner can speed things up a lot.

1

Join the WPC

Sign up as a WPC member at wirelesspowerconsortium.com. Fees range from $2,500 (small firm) to $15,000+ (big maker). Your member level decides your access to working groups and early spec updates.

Duration: 1-2 weeks for application approval.

2

Product Registration

Submit your product details through the WPC member portal. Include design specs, power class (5W to 65W), coil setup, and target device types.

Duration: 1-3 days.

3

Sample Preparation

Make samples that match your final product exactly —same parts, same PCB, same firmware. Any difference between test samples and final units will void your cert.

Duration: 2-6 weeks depending on product complexity.

4

Authorized Laboratory Testing

Send samples to a WPC-accredited Qi test lab. Tests cover power efficiency, FOD, temperature control, EMF, and cross-device fit. Browse our Qi-certified wireless charger collection to see products that have passed these tests.

Duration: 2-6 weeks depending on test complexity and lab scheduling.

5

Certification Review

WPC reviews test results and support docs. If all rules are met, your product gets Qi cert and is listed in the official Qi certified product database.

Duration: 1-2 weeks.

6

Logo Activation

Once certified, you receive the official Qi logo files and guidelines for proper usage. You can now legally use the Qi logo on your products, packaging, and marketing materials.

4. Detailed Testing Requirements

Qi testing is thorough. It covers all parts of wireless power. All tests follow the latest Qi spec.

Test Category Key Requirements Failure Impact
Power Transfer Efficiency. Must maintain ≥80% efficiency at rated power levels across all operating conditions. Excessive heat generation, device damage, failed certification.
Foreign Object Detection (FOD) Must detect and respond to metal objects (coins, keys) within specified time limits; varies by power class. Fire risk, safety hazard, product recall.
Temperature Control. Surface temperature must stay below 40°C under all charging conditions; coil temperature limits apply. Safety hazard, device damage, failed certification.
EMF Emissions. Must comply with ICNIRP guidelines for human exposure to electromagnetic fields. Regulatory violation, market access denial.
Interoperability Testing. Must work correctly with reference Qi receivers and at least three different certified transmitter devices. Failed certification, interoperability issues.
Power Class Compliance. Must meet all requirements specific to declared power class (BPP, EPP, MPP) Certification denial, re-testing required.
Coil Alignment. Must maintain stable power transfer within specified alignment tolerances. Intermittent charging, user complaints.

Testing Tip

Pre-compliance testing at independent labs can save thousands. Do this before official WPC testing. Find and fix issues early. This avoids failed tests and costly re-testing.

7. Qi2 vs Qi Certification Differences

Qi2 launched in 2023 as a big step up in wireless charging. Here are the key differences between Qi (v1.x) and Qi2 to help with product planning.

Feature Qi (BPP/EPP) Qi2 (MPP)
Magnetic Alignment. No (relies on free positioning) Yes (MagSafe-compatible ring)
Efficiency. 65-75% typical. 80%+ with aligned magnets.
Power Output. Up to 15W (EPP) Up to 15W baseline, extensible to 100W+.
Apple Support. iPhone 8 - iPhone 14 (7.5W) iPhone 12+ full 15W (Made for iPhone)
Android Support. Samsung, Google, others (varies) Growing Android support expected.
Testing Complexity. Moderate. Higher (includes magnetic testing)
Certification Cost. $5,000-$15,000. $10,000-$20,000.
Market Demand. Established, declining. Growing rapidly.

Which Standard Should You Target?

Qi (BPP/EPP): Best for budget chargers, car mounts, furniture integration, and markets where Qi2 accessories aren't prevalent. Lower certification costs.

Qi2 (MPP): Best for premium chargers targeting iPhone 12+ users, magnetic accessories (power banks, stands), and future-proofing your product line.

Many manufacturers now pursue dual certification. They offer products that support both Qi and Qi2 for maximum market coverage. Our Qi-certified wireless chargers include options in both categories.

8. Common Certification Mistakes to Avoid

Based on our work with dozens of brands, here are the most common Qi cert mistakes and how to avoid them:

Mistake #1: Sample-Production Mismatch

The #1 cause of cert failure. Test samples must match final units exactly. Small changes like a different MOSFET or coil tweak mean re-testing. Lock your BOM before testing starts.

Mistake #2: Insufficient FOD Testing

FOD is one of the most failed test areas. Test with coins, paper clips, keys, and metal plates at various spots. Don't assume your FOD algorithm works without thorough testing.

Mistake #3: Underestimating Thermal Design

Temperature limits are strict —surface must stay under 40°C. Good thermal design needs careful PCB layout, parts, and enclosure. Test thermal performance under worst-case conditions with high heat and full load.

Mistake #4: Wrong Power Class Selection

Claiming more power than your product can deliver is a serious violation. Be careful with your power class claim. You can always add higher power modes later.

Mistake #5: Incomplete Documentation

WPC cert needs detailed docs. Include schematics, BOM, firmware version, test notes, and user manuals. Missing or incomplete docs will slow down your review.

Mistake #6: Ignoring Regional Requirements

Qi cert is separate from FCC, CE, and RoHS marks. You need both. Plan your timing to align with or come before Qi testing. See our US/EU certifications guide for details.

10. Qi Logo Usage Rules

Once certified, follow strict rules when using the Qi logo. The Qi logo usage rules are part of your WPC membership deal.

Use only on certified products listed in the WPC database.

Display the logo exactly as provided by WPC (no modifications to colors, proportions, or design)

Include Qi logo in product packaging, user manuals, and marketing materials.

The Qi logo must be clearly visible on the product itself or its retail packaging.

Never use on non-certified products or accessories (including bundled cables)

Don't claim "Qi compatible" or "Works with Qi" without official certification.

Don't use Qi logos in ways that suggest endorsement by WPC beyond your specific certified products.
