Shipping from China Guide 2026

The definitive guide to importing chargers from China. Learn about Incoterms, freight options, shipping costs, customs clearance, and how to calculate total landed costs.

May 3, 2026 9 min read

1. Why Understanding Shipping Matters

Shipping costs can account for 5-30% of your total product cost when importing from China. Miscalculating these costs is one of the most common mistakes new importers make, leading to margin compression or pricing that's uncompetitive in the market.

At WOWOHCOOL, we've helped over 500 brands navigate the complexities of importing from China since 2013. Our logistics team coordinates daily with freight forwarders across all major shipping routes, ensuring competitive rates and reliable delivery schedules for wireless chargers and all our products.

Shipping Cost Impact

  • • Shipping averages 8-15% of total landed cost for standard orders
  • • Air freight can add $0.50-2.00 per unit vs. sea freight
  • • Express shipping (DHL/FedEx) adds $2-5 per unit for small orders
  • • Dimensional weight pricing can double shipping costs for light products

2. Understanding Incoterms

Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) define who is responsible for what during the shipping process. Understanding these terms is essential for accurate cost calculation and risk management.

Incoterm Name Risk Transfer Best For
EXW Ex Works Seller's premises Experienced importers with own logistics
FOB Free on Board Port of loading Most common for sea freight
CIF Cost Insurance Freight Port of loading Buyer wants insurance included
DDP Delivered Duty Paid Buyer's premises Turnkey solution, higher price
DAP Delivered at Place Named place Buyer pays duties separately

Recommended Incoterms for Chargers

FOB Shenzhen / FOB Guangzhou: Most common and recommended for most importers. The seller handles factory preparation and port handling; you arrange main carriage and insurance.

DDP (Delivered Duty Paid): Best for new importers who want a single invoice covering everything. The seller handles all logistics including duties.

EXW: Only recommended if you have established relationships with freight forwarders and can arrange all logistics efficiently.

3. Shipping Options: Sea, Air & Express

Choosing the right shipping method depends on your order volume, urgency, and budget. Here's a detailed comparison:

Sea Freight (20GP / 40GP / 40HC)

Transit Times

  • • China to US West Coast: 12-18 days
  • • China to US East Coast: 25-35 days
  • • China to Europe: 25-35 days
  • • China to Australia: 15-25 days

Container Capacities

  • • 20GP: ~25-28 CBM (ideal for 2,000-3,000 chargers)
  • • 40GP: ~55-58 CBM (ideal for 4,000-6,000 chargers)
  • • 40HC: ~65-68 CBM (ideal for 5,000-8,000 chargers)

Estimated Costs (2026)

  • • China to US: $1,800-4,500 (20GP), $2,500-6,000 (40GP)
  • • China to EU: $1,500-3,500 (20GP), $2,000-5,000 (40GP)
  • • BAF (Bunker Adjustment): $200-800 per container
  • • CAF (Currency Adjustment): 5-15% of freight rate

Air Freight

Transit Times

  • • China to US: 5-7 days
  • • China to Europe: 4-6 days
  • • China to Australia: 3-5 days

Charges

  • • Chargeable weight: Max of actual vs. dimensional
  • • Dimensional factor: L×W×H (cm) ÷ 6000
  • • Minimum charge: Often applies

Estimated Costs (2026)

  • • General rate: $3.50-6.00 per kg
  • • Minimum chargeable: $120-200
  • • Fuel surcharge: $0.30-0.60 per kg
  • • Security surcharge: $0.10-0.20 per kg

Example: 500kg shipment = $2,000-3,500 air freight

Express Courier (DHL / FedEx / UPS)

Best for samples, small orders, or urgent shipments. Door-to-door service with customs clearance included.

Estimated Costs (2026)

  • • Per kg rate: $8-15 (depending on zone)
  • • Minimum shipment: 0.5-1 kg
  • • Fuel surcharge: 15-20% of base rate
  • • Customs clearance: Usually included

Example: 10kg shipment = $150-250 express

Best for: Samples under 100kg, urgent reorders, trade show inventory

4. Calculating Total Landed Cost

Total Landed Cost (TLC) is the complete cost of getting your products to your warehouse, including all fees and duties. Here's how to calculate it:

Landed Cost Formula

TLC = Product Cost + Freight + Insurance + Customs Duties + Port Fees + Last Mile Delivery

  • Product Cost: FOB price from supplier
  • Freight: Sea freight ($0.05-0.15/unit) or air freight ($0.30-0.80/unit)
  • Insurance: Typically 0.2-0.5% of cargo value (CIF)
  • Customs Duties: HTS code rate × CIF value (US: 8-10% for chargers)
  • Port Fees: Terminal handling, port congestion surcharges
  • Last Mile: Trucking to your warehouse

Example Calculation: 5,000 Wireless Chargers

Product Cost (FOB) $15,000
Sea Freight (40GP) $3,000 ($0.60/unit)
Insurance (0.3%) $54
CIF Value $18,054
Customs Duty (9%) $1,625
Port Handling & Fees $350
Last Mile Delivery $400
TOTAL LANDED COST $20,429
Cost Per Unit $4.09 (vs. $3.00 FOB)

Key Insight: Shipping and duties added 36% to the product cost. Always factor this into your pricing strategy!

5. Customs Clearance Process

Customs clearance is often the most confusing part of importing. Understanding the process helps avoid delays and unexpected costs.

Required Documentation

  • • Commercial Invoice: Detailed invoice with HS codes, values, Incoterms
  • • Packing List: Contents, quantities, weights, carton dimensions
  • • Bill of Lading (B/L): Ocean bill for sea freight, air waybill for air
  • • Certificate of Origin: Required for preferential tariffs (FTA countries)
  • • Product Specifications: Technical details for HS code classification
  • • FDA Registration: Required for certain electronics entering the US

HS Codes for Chargers

Product HS Code US Duty EU Duty
Wireless Chargers 8504.40.95 0% 2.5%
USB Chargers (5V) 8504.40.85 2.5% 2.5%
Fast Chargers (PD) 8504.40.95 0% 2.5%
Car Chargers 8504.40.85 2.5% 2.5%

Note: Duties may vary. Always verify current rates with a customs broker.

Common Customs Issues

  • • Undervaluation: Never undervalue goods. Customs can seize shipments and impose penalties.
  • • Wrong HS Code: Misclassification can result in delays, fines, or duty adjustments.
  • • Missing Documentation: Incomplete paperwork extends clearance time by days or weeks.
  • • FDA Requirements: Electronic devices may require FDA registration before import.

6. Choosing a Freight Forwarder

A good freight forwarder can save you money and headaches. Here's what to look for:

Essential Criteria

  • • Licensed by FIATA or similar body
  • • Experience with electronics/chargers
  • • China office with English-speaking staff
  • • Network of reliable carriers
  • • Transparent pricing (no hidden fees)
  • • Responsive communication

Questions to Ask

  • • What are all your fees (including surcharges)?
  • • How do you handle customs clearance?
  • • What's your typical transit time guarantee?
  • • Do you offer cargo insurance?
  • • How do you handle claims or delays?
  • • Can you provide references from similar clients?

7. Shipping Tips for Chargers

Optimize Packaging

Standard retail packaging can be compressed. Individual boxes nested together in cartons maximize container space. For chargers, a 40GP holds approximately 8,000-12,000 units with standard retail packaging.

Consolidate Shipments

If ordering from multiple suppliers, consolidate shipments into one container. LCL (Less than Container Load) consolidation saves money but adds handling time and risk.

Book During Off-Peak

Shipping rates spike during September-November (pre-holiday) and Chinese New Year. Book containers 4-6 weeks in advance during peak season to secure space and rates.

Insure Your Cargo

Marine cargo insurance costs 0.1-0.5% of cargo value but protects against loss, damage, and delays. Standard coverage excludes rough handling and moisture damage—get all-risk coverage for peace of mind.

Plan for Port Congestion

Major ports experience delays during peak seasons. Los Angeles/Long Beach currently averages 3-7 days of waiting time. Build this buffer into your inventory planning or consider alternative ports.

Conclusion: Plan Ahead for Success

Shipping from China doesn't have to be complicated. Understanding Incoterms, knowing your shipping options, and accurately calculating landed costs are the foundations of successful importing.

Whether you're importing power chargers, wireless chargers, or car chargers, the principles remain the same: plan ahead, work with reliable partners, and always factor shipping into your pricing.
